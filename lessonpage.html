What Really is the Stock Market?


The stock market refers to public markets that exist for issuing, buying and selling stocks that trade on a stock exchange or over-the-counter. Stocks, also known as equities, represent fractional ownership in a company, and the stock market is a place where investors can buy and sell ownership of such investible assets. An efficiently functioning stock market is considered critical to economic development, as it gives companies the ability to quickly access capital from the public.

Wow that looks scary doesn't it? Now you must be wondering, what in the world does that mean? Well first, let's understand what a market means. A market is a place maybe in your neighborhood, near your school, in your local shopping mall, or even on the internet where the exchange of things takes place. Of course, the phrase "exchange of things" is pretty vague isn't it? The "exchange of things", as far as the 21st century is concerned is almost always the exchange of goods and services for money and a good could be anything ranging from pizza, to a smartphone, to even a hugh bouncy castle and a service could be Stockeezy (the tool you're using right now), a dental checkup, a car servicing, and a lot more.
